The Narrative

I am a researcher and educator with 12 years of pedagogical experience, specializing in the Social Science of Knowledge Infrastructures. My research explores the "Mobility of Knowledge"—specifically how informal, underground technical expertise (BBS/Hacker Culture) transitions into formal academic institutions.

Currently based in Vienna, I am investigating how Academic Capitalism influences the assessment of digital expertise.

Key Research & Digital Works

From BBS to Blackboard (Master Thesis)

STS Digital Discourse Analysis Hacker Culture

A socio-technical analysis of Phrack Magazine (1985-2016). Investigated how early digital forums functioned as alternative epistemic cultures. This project won a publication award and mapped the "co-production" of technical expertise before the era of formal cybersecurity education.

Theological Studies Online Museum

HTML/CSS UX Design Public Engagement

Award: 30/30 Grade. Designed an interactive digital exhibit bridging historical theological archives with modern tourism. A proof-of-concept for innovative teaching and societal engagement through digital platforms.

Social Justice Mapping (ArcGIS)

Big Data NAGPRA Geospatial Analysis

Visualized the American Indian Repatriation Movement using ArcGIS. This project analyzed the intersection of environmental history, legal policy, and digital data management.
